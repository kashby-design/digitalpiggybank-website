# Content brief: Age-by-age allowance & chores pillar guide

> **Note:** The pillar page itself was built in Phase 5 at [/guides/allowance-and-chores-by-age](../../src/pages/guides/allowance-and-chores-by-age.astro). This brief documents the strategy behind it and the work needed before publishing publicly.

## Target keyword
- **Primary:** allowance and chores by age
- **Secondary:** age appropriate chores chart, how much allowance by age, kids money guide by age, when to start allowance
- **Search intent:** Informational / pillar-page intent. Parents want the *whole picture* in one place.

## Goal
Be the single most useful page on the internet for "what should my [age] do?" — a definitive reference that earns links and converts blog readers into app users.

## Audience
Parents of kids 4–12 who've decided they want to teach money but don't know where to start. Often searching at midnight after a frustrating chore-related argument with their kid.

## Format
- **Type:** Pillar guide page (not a blog post). Lives at `/guides/`.
- **Length:** ~2,500 words. Built into 7 anchored sections with a sticky-ish TOC.
- **Layout:** No traditional sidebar. TOC card up top. Each section ~300–400 words. Heavy internal linking to deeper posts.

## Outline (already built)

The current draft has:
1. The mindset shift
2. Ages 4–5: introducing money
3. Ages 6–7: real responsibility
4. Ages 8–9: chore-pay systems
5. Ages 10–12: small-job structure
6. Tools by age (comparison table)
7. Common mistakes
8. CTA section

This structure is solid. The text needs one editorial pass before publishing publicly (see below).

## Pre-launch editorial pass

Before promoting the pillar:
- [ ] Replace the placeholder hero image with a custom illustration (currently using `_placeholder.svg`).
- [ ] Add 1–2 parent-experience anecdotes per age section. Real stories outperform stats.
- [ ] Add 2–3 cited stats (e.g., the Minnesota chores study, BYU LeBaron research, Marshmallow Test). Each cite gets a real link.
- [ ] Recruit one early reader who's a parent of a 6-year-old and one of a 10-year-old. Get their unblocked feedback on whether the advice feels real.
- [ ] Add a printable PDF checklist version (lead magnet) — **deferred**, see Phase 5 plan risk note about missing email infrastructure.

## Success metrics
- **3-month goal:** indexed and ranking somewhere on page 1–3 for "allowance by age" or "age appropriate chores"
- **6-month goal:** top 10 for the primary keyword, ≥ 5 inbound links from parenting/family blogs
- **12-month goal:** 1,500+ organic visits/month to this single page; demonstrable downstream funnel into the app

## Distribution

When this is editorial-ready, push it on:
- The site nav (link from the homepage hero or a top-level "Guides" entry)
- The blog index page (pin it above the post grid)
- A first-touch email or newsletter (if/when email is set up)
- Parenting subreddits where the topic is on-charter (r/Parenting allowance threads, r/raisingkids)
- Optional: outreach to 5–10 parenting bloggers with personalized notes — this is the kind of resource that earns links if pitched well
