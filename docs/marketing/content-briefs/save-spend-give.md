# Content brief: Save / Spend / Give system (already drafted as blog post)

> **Note:** A blog-post-length version was drafted in Phase 5 at [/blog/save-spend-give-jars-for-kids](../../src/content/blog/save-spend-give-jars-for-kids.md). This brief documents the strategic intent and lists what's needed to extend it into a higher-converting standalone resource page.

## Target keyword
- **Primary:** save spend give jars for kids
- **Secondary:** three jar system kids, save spend give chart, teaching kids to give, kids money buckets
- **Search intent:** Informational, with a clear next-step opportunity. Parents searching this term are ready to set up a system; many will download a printable.

## Goal
- Rank top 5 for "save spend give jars for kids" within 6 months
- Drive printable downloads (lead magnet, **deferred** until email capture is set up)
- Funnel high-intent traffic into trying the digital piggy bank app

## Audience
Parents of kids 4–10 who've heard about the three-jar system but want a real setup walkthrough, not just a Pinterest infographic.

## Format options

There are two ways to ship this:

**Option A — Blog post only (current state):** The 800-word post at `/blog/save-spend-give-jars-for-kids` covers the basics. Good for blog-grid presence. Less likely to rank as the "definitive" page.

**Option B — Resource page + blog post:** Promote a separate `/save-spend-give` page (or similar) that's deeper, includes the printable, and serves as the canonical reference. The blog post links to it. **This is the recommended evolution.**

The brief below assumes Option B.

## Outline (Option B — resource page, ~1,800 words)

1. **Lede (150 words):** open with the pitch — three jars, three lessons, five minutes to set up.
2. **Why three jars instead of one (200 words):** the three jobs of money, with research cite (U of Arizona).
3. **The three jars explained (450 words):** Save / Spend / Give — what goes in each, with the *rule* for each jar. Include 1–2 specific examples per jar.
4. **How much in each — by age (250 words):** Suggested splits, including a table mapping age + amount → split.
5. **Setting it up in 5 minutes (200 words):** Numbered checklist. Include the printable jar labels (PDF download).
6. **When jars stop working (200 words):** Realistic transition signals — bigger goals, digital spending, awkward bill denominations.
7. **Common mistakes (200 words):** Bulleted list with brief explanations.
8. **A note for parents who didn't grow up with this (100 words):** Reframing for parents who feel weird about the system.
9. **CTA + next steps (50 words):** Soft pitch to the digital piggy bank for when jars stop scaling.

## Lead magnet (deferred)

The plan includes a printable PDF — printable jar labels + a tracking sheet. **Defer until email capture is set up** (see Phase 5 plan risks). When ready:
- Printable PDF with: 3 jar labels (SAVE, SPEND, GIVE), a goal-tracking sheet, a weekly split worksheet
- Email gate (ConvertKit, Buttondown, or MailerLite)
- Confirmation email with the PDF + a 5-email onboarding sequence about teaching kids money

## Success metrics
- **3-month goal:** indexed for the primary keyword, top 20
- **6-month goal:** top 5 for the primary keyword
- **12-month goal:** 500+ visits/month, ≥ 200 lead-magnet downloads/month (post-email-setup)

## Risks / honest notes
- The save / spend / give concept is taught by lots of brands already (Dave Ramsey, Money Smart Family, school curricula). To stand out, lean hard into specifics: exact rules per jar, age-by-age splits, realistic transition advice. Don't write another generic explainer.
- Resist over-pitching the app. The lesson works fine with jars. The app is for *after* jars stop scaling — name that explicitly.
